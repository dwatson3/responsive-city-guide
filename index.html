My Top Six Favorites

Established in 1938, the Stern Grove Festival Concerts is the oldest admission-free summer performing arts festival of its kind in America.

The San Francisco Ferry Building is a terminal for ferries that travel across the San Francisco Bay. Some of the city's best known farmers markets and restaurants are held here.

The Tonga Room & Hurricane Bar has delighted guests with its tropical decor, decadent libations, rainfall storms, and Asian cuisine since 1945.

Ocean Beach is a 3.5-mile stretch of white beach with few tourists and no highrises. A great place for bonfires, surfing, jogging, and hanging out with friends.

AT&T Park has proudly been hosting Giants baseball games. Enjoy a beer, hotdog, and the live action!

Once called "the bridge that couldn't be built," today it is one of the seven wonders of the modern world. San Francisco's most famous landmark, the bridge opened in 1937 after a four-year struggle against relentless winds, fog, rock and treacherous tides.
